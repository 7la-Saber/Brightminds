More About Stress

Stress is the body’s reaction to feeling threatened or under pressure. It can be triggered by various situations, such as work, academic pressure, financial issues, or major life changes. When you perceive a threat or a high-pressure situation, your body releases hormones like adrenaline and cortisol, preparing you for a “fight or flight” response.

How to Know if You Are Stressed

There are many signs that may be symptoms of stress as it manifests itself in various ways, affecting both your body and mind. Here are a few:

  • Irritability: Feeling easily frustrated or angry.
  • Anxiety: Constant worry or feeling overwhelmed.
  • Depression: Persistent sadness or lack of interest in activities.
  • Headaches: Frequent or severe headaches.
  • Muscle Tension: Stiffness or pain in muscles, especially in the neck and shoulders.
  • Fatigue: Feeling constantly tired or exhausted.
  • Poor Concentration: Trouble focusing or making decisions.
  • Negative Thinking: Persistent negative thoughts or pessimism.
